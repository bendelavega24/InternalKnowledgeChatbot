It contains 3 questions per retrieval category:
- direct
- cross_doc
- false_premise
- negative

Total test cases: 12


CATEGORY CRITERIA
-----------------

1. direct
   Meaning:
   The answer should be retrieved mainly from one expected source document.

   Pass criteria:
   - At least one expected source appears in the final retrieved context.
   - At least one expected keyword appears in the final retrieved context.

2. cross_doc
   Meaning:
   The question is designed to require related context from two or more documents.

   Pass criteria:
   - At least one expected source appears in the final retrieved context.
   - At least one expected keyword appears in the final retrieved context.
   - Better result if multiple expected sources appear.

3. false_premise
   Meaning:
   The question contains a wrong assumption.

   Pass criteria:
   - Retrieval should find corrective context from the right source.
   - It should retrieve text showing the assumption is wrong.

4. negative
   Meaning:
   The question asks for information that should not exist in the documents.

   Pass criteria:
   - The fake or sensitive requested detail should not appear in the final retrieved context.
   - This helps test that the system does not support hallucinated answers.
